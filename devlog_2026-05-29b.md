## Bug Fixes

### Flow alert markers not rendering on SPX chart
Root cause: `_flowMarkers` array was populated from the `/api/flow_alerts` fetch, but `applyFlowMarkers()` was never called after that — and even when called, `priceCharts['SPX']` didn't exist yet because price fetch runs in parallel.

Fix: poll for `priceCharts['SPX']?.markersPrimitive` every 200ms and apply markers as soon as the chart is ready.

### LightweightCharts v5 `setMarkers` API
`setMarkers` was removed from `ISeriesApi` in LWC v5. Replaced with `LWC.createSeriesMarkers(candleSeries, [])` which returns a primitive. The primitive's `setMarkers()` method is then called with the combined session + flow markers array.

### Session markers and flow markers overwriting each other
Both `applySessionMarkers` and `applyFlowMarkers` were calling `setMarkers` independently, each wiping the other. Fixed by merging both arrays in `applySessionMarkers` and making `applyFlowMarkers` delegate to it.

### numpy 2.4.6 segfault (Python 3.12 / Windows)
Exception code `0xc0000005` (access violation) inside `python312.dll`. Caused by numpy 2.x heap corruption under multi-threaded workload (pandas GEX parsing in one thread while Flask + WebSocket run in others). Downgraded to `numpy==1.26.4` — stable with Python 3.12 on Windows.

## Live Trading Observations (SPX 2026-05-29)

All observations from the live dashboard during market hours.

### 10:58 — PUT SELL block
Two simultaneous 2,200-contract deep ITM put sells at 7600 and 7605 (delta 0.91–0.94). Total ~$14M premium. Bullish signal — dealers forced to buy futures to unwind short delta from previously held puts. Price reversed up from that point.

### 11:44 — Synthetic short
4,030 CALL SELL + 4,018 PUT BUY at 7600 strike, one second apart. Same size, same strike, same expiry = synthetic short. Caused a brief sharp dip as dealers sold futures on both legs simultaneously. Absorbed within minutes as broader call buying continued.

### 12:30–13:00 — Gamma squeeze
0DTE call line went from +4.36B to +8.46B in ~30 minutes. Put line flipped from negative to +0.34B. Both positive simultaneously = maximum dealer buy pressure from both sides. Classic self-reinforcing gamma squeeze:
- Price up → calls go deeper ITM → dealer delta increases → dealers buy more futures → price up

### Close — Pin at 7600
Despite +8.46B of 0DTE call pressure, SPX pinned at 7590 into close. Call wall at 7600 held as resistance. The 4,030-contract synthetic short from 11:44 sitting at exactly 7600 was likely a contributing factor. ES had already broken 7603 intraday but SPX cash couldn't follow.

The 0DTE cumulative delta chart proved its value today. The call line going vertical 30 minutes before close was a real-time signal that dealers were mechanically forced into buying.
